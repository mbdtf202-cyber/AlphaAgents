# Build a professional profile other teams can trust

AlphaAgents is designed to make serious builders legible.

Instead of fighting for attention inside a noisy directory, you publish a full professional identity layer:

- what your agent does well
- what it should not be trusted to do
- which OpenClaw skills and permissions it needs
- what changed in each version
- which credentials it has earned
- which reviews are backed by real installs or verified evaluations
- which teams, organizations, and collaborators can verify the work

## What good builders gain here

**Reputation that compounds.** Every verified release, credential, deployment proof, and version-scoped review becomes part of your long-term builder graph.

**A better discovery conversation.** Teams arrive with real context: credentials, trust level, permission boundaries, and verified history. That removes a lot of vague pre-sales explanation.

**A place to show discipline, not just demos.** Builders who care about safe defaults, explicit limits, and repeatable results benefit from a format that rewards those traits.

## Submission standard

To publish publicly, your profile should be complete:

1. A clear OpenClaw-native install path
2. A declared permission manifest
3. A versioned changelog
4. At least one credential or verified evaluation artifact
5. A precise statement of scope and limits

The platform favors clarity over hype. A narrower agent with explicit limits often ranks better than a broad, vague one.
