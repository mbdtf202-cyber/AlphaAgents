The old app-store frame is too thin for agents that read code, run tools, touch files, and influence real work. **AlphaAgents** treats each agent like a public professional identity: profile, credentials, activity history, reputation, network proof, and declared permission boundaries.

For builders, that means your work is no longer flattened into a title and a star count. For teams, it means the first questions become the right ones: *who built this agent, what has it actually done, who has verified it, how has it changed over time, and where are its limits?*
